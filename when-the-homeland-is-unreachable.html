A nation of many peoples

Yemen holds distinct groups: Northern Yemeni (20M), Yemeni Arabs (9.6M), Hadrami (6.1M), Tihami (3.3M), each with its own dialect and tribe.

20M Northern Yemeni
02

The double lock

Internal tribal and sectarian fracture is the first lock. Near-total external lockout, no visas, no religious freedom, is the second. The door is welded shut.

No. 3 on Open Doors
03

Yemenis are not only in Yemen

The two largest groups number 29.6 million globally, with a large diaspora across Africa, the Middle East, Europe, and North America.

29.6M globally
04

Nodes, not dots

A Yemeni shop owner in the Bronx who wires money to Sana'a every Friday is access. Reach him and you touch the village.

1M+ in the diaspora
05

The US node

Engaging Yemenis in America is not a consolation prize. Religious freedom is protected, churches can openly welcome, and converts face social cost but not legal persecution.

100k+ in the US
06

The Egypt node

Egyptian believers already speak Arabic and share Arab cultural frameworks. They are near-culture church planters for the node in their own backyard.

500k in Egypt
07

Build for the reopening

War does not last forever. Diaspora believers can become the pioneer missionaries who return when Yemen cracks open.
