What the map tells you

One solid block of red.

Open almost any missions database and Turkey reads as a near-monolith: 99% Sunni Muslim. Strategy gets built on that single number.

What it hides

It was never one block.

Somewhere between 4.5 million and 20 million Alevis live inside that red. They broke from Sunni Islam roughly 700 years ago. For centuries they survived by hiding (taqiyya), so the data never saw them.

Who the Alevis actually are

If you assumed they were Sunni, almost everything you would say next is aimed at the wrong target.

01
They do not attend mosques.
Alevi worship happens in the cemevi, a gathering house, not the mosque. The five daily prayers and the pilgrimage to Mecca are not their practice.
02
They do not veil.
Men and women worship together. The social rules an outsider expects of a "traditional Muslim" community simply do not hold here.
03
They drink wine in worship.
The cem ceremony can include wine, music, and dance, practices that would be unthinkable in the Sunni framework strategists assumed.
04
Their holy instrument is the saz.
The long-necked lute is so central that Alevis call it "the Quran with strings." Truth is sung, not just recited.
05
They broke away 700 years ago.
Alevism diverged from Sunni legalism centuries ago, carrying a mystical, inward stream of faith that looks almost nothing like its neighbors.
06
They survived by being invisible.
Massacres at Maras, Corum, and Sivas from the 1970s to the 1990s taught a hard lesson: stay hidden. Which is exactly why the databases missed them.
Why it matters

Bad data does not just slow strategy. It misdiagnoses the heart of a people.

When we misidentify a group, we answer questions they were never asking, and we miss the questions they actually carry. To reach the Alevis, you do not argue them out of Sunni Islam. You meet them where they already are.
