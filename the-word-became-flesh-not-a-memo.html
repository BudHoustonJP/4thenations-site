Skeuomorphism, or how new things get adopted

Strip the costume and watch what stays

Apple shipped the first iPhone calendar with fake leather stitching. None of it did anything. It was there so your thumb would trust a sheet of glass. Once people trusted the glass, the stitching came off. Raymond Loewy called the rule MAYA: Most Advanced Yet Acceptable. Drag the slider to remove the familiar wrapper and see what was structure and what was scaffolding.

Kernel and shell

Same kernel, three native shells

Borrow the language from computing. The kernel is the core that does not change: the Lordship of Christ, his death and resurrection, repentance and faith, a gathered body baptized and breaking bread (Acts 2:42). The shell is the form that carries it: where the church gathers, who leads, what it sings. Switch shells and watch the kernel hold.

Kernel intact
Pipe organ, pews, a robed clergyman, Sunday at 11. Every one of these is negotiable shell. The kernel at the center has not moved.

The precedent

Paul's playbook: what flexed and what never did

Paul spent his life on this principle, and the early church fought its first serious controversy over it. Open each case and see which layer was being handled.

Timothy would work among Jews, so Paul removed a barrier of foreignness before it could block the message. The form bent to the audience. Nothing in the gospel changed.
When the same act was demanded as a condition of belonging, Paul refused. A shell requirement was trying to attach itself to the kernel, and he would not let the wrapper become the price of Christ.
At the Areopagus Paul carried the message in the hearers' own literature and categories. He became all things to all people to save some (1 Cor 9:22). The wrapper was theirs. The claim was his.
Did Gentiles need the Jewish wrapper, circumcision and food laws, to receive the Jewish Messiah? Peter asked why anyone would lay on the disciples a yoke that neither they nor their ancestors could bear (Acts 15:10). Keep the kernel, drop the shell, and do not make it harder for those turning to God (Acts 15:19).
Everything flexed except one thing. Paul would not soften Christ crucified, even knowing it was a stumbling block. The offense of the cross is required. The offense of foreignness is one we invented.

Two ways to wreck it

The road and the two ditches

This cuts both ways. Make the shell foreign and people reject the wrapper thinking they rejected Christ. Make the shell so accommodating the kernel disappears, and Christ is added to the old system rather than seated above it. Tap each zone of the road.

The contextualization audit

Run it with your team, out loud

Four questions, form by form. Check each one off as your team actually answers it, not as you read it.

For each requirement in your current strategy, can you name whether it is kernel or shell, and defend the call from Scripture rather than habit?
What are you asking your people group to adopt, in gospel and in church form, that came from your culture rather than the New Testament?
Where is your church shell filtering out the people most open to the kernel: the non-literate, the poor, the household head who cannot come out alone? A text-first shell filters out an oral culture. An individual-decision shell breaks the household pattern (Acts 16:31-34).
What is your plan to remove your shell so the church can reproduce in its own? A healthy indigenous church is one where the missionary's shell is gone and the people's own shell carries the unchanging kernel, reproducibly, without us.
