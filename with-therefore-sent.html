Two verbs, one life

Most of us were taught the Christian life is sequential: first you grow, then you go. That framing keeps a lot of people stuck.

01

One posture, two verbs

Mark 3:14 holds the whole frame: appointed to be with him and to be sent out. The with-ness produces the sent-ness.

Mark 3:14
02

With three at once

A fully sent life means being with God, with the lost, and with the church at the same time. Remove any one and sent-ness breaks down.

3 spheres
03

With the lost

Discipleship quietly skips this. Pipelines produce insularity and call it maturity. Being with the lost is where urgency stops being theoretical.

04

With the church confirms

Antioch did not rubber-stamp a stranger. They had watched Barnabas and Saul for years before the Spirit said set them apart.

Acts 13:2-3
05

Gifting shapes trajectory

Apostle, prophet, evangelist, shepherd, teacher: not offices but how sent-ness is distributed. All five need all three spheres; the weighting differs.

5 gifts, Ephesians 4
06

Your vocation is the vehicle

The engineer in Central Asia, the nurse with her patients, the manager forming his team. Vocation is not secular. It is the calling.

“You are not sent to do God's work for him. You are sent to do God's work with him.”

“Sent-ness rides on the back of the life you are already living.”

Every gift. Every life. Every vocation. Sent.
