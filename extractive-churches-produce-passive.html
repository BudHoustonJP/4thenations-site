Clarity for the Nations · Church & Culture

More curriculum than any generation in history. Fewer disciples. The problem is the design.

Borrowing the extractive-versus-inclusive lens from Why Nations Fail, this essay argues that most churches are structured to gather from their members, not to develop them. A priest is someone who has been handed the work.

The frame, from Why Nations Fail

Which way does the ministry flow?

Acemoglu and Robinson showed that extractive institutions pull resources toward a narrow elite, while inclusive institutions distribute participation broadly. Overlay the church and watch the arrows. Toggle between the two designs.

Extractive
Attendance, giving, and volunteer labor flow inward to a professional core. It can look healthy and even grow fast, but it produces reliable attenders and tired volunteers.

The engine, in one verse

Four links in one chain

Paul made the mechanism explicit: what you have heard from me, entrust to faithful people who will be able to teach others (2 Timothy 2:2). Tap each link. The chain only works if every link passes the work down rather than hoarding it.

Watch a passive attender become an operating priest

A benevolence ministry runs entirely through two paid staff, so it can never serve more families than two people can carry. Then the pastor hands the whole thing to Marcus, a forklift operator who has never led anything. Step through the Tuesdays.
