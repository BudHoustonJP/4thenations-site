01

The generic dashboard trap

A team reports gospel shares and baptisms on the same form. Healthy shares, zero baptisms, month after month. Whether that is a problem depends entirely on where they are.

02

Same numbers, different verdict

Zero baptisms at six months in early evangelism may be right. The same numbers for seven years means something is deeply wrong, or the tracking is hiding it.

6 months vs 7 years
03

Phase-specific metrics

Early evangelism needs granular data: prayer hours, conversations, who is sharing, the response. Reproducing churches need a completely different dashboard of leadership and generational depth.

04

What gets inspected gets expected

Your team optimizes for whatever you ask about. Ask 'how many conversations?' and they chase conversations. Ask 'what did you learn?' and they think strategically.

05

Find the flow, remove the drag

The goal is not to manufacture movement but to find where the Spirit is already moving and get out of the way. Then your job shifts to removing barriers.

06

A resolution worth keeping

Maybe this year's goal is not a new initiative. It is to track the 3 to 5 metrics that signal progress to your next phase, and actually look at them every quarter.
