01

The numbers that rewrite strategy

New UN data: 45% live in cities, 36% in towns, 19% rural. So 81% of humanity is urban-like, but 96% of the world's cities have fewer than 1 million people.

45 / 36 / 19, and 96% of cities under 1M
02

From dot to network of nodes

People live multi-local lives across origin villages, market towns, regional cities, metros, cross-border cities, diaspora, and online. The question shifts from 'where do they live?' to 'which nodes give the best leverage?'

7 node types
03

Towns: the invisible third

Towns house more than a third of humanity and are the most common settlement type in 71 countries, yet they are nearly invisible in mission portfolios. Often the most strategic first node.

a third of humanity, most common in 71 countries
04

Seven countries shape everything

India, Nigeria, Pakistan, DR Congo, Egypt, Bangladesh, and Ethiopia will add over 500 million city residents by 2050, more than half of global city growth.

plus 500 million city residents by 2050
05

Collaboration becomes non-negotiable

No single organization can work all the nodes for a people. A multi-node strategy forces shared research, agreed priorities, and regular communication across ministries.

06

Co-architects, not implementers

Believers in those seven growth countries already have relational access to nodes outsiders never could. Do not recruit them as 'local partners.' Invite them as co-designers.

07

Track strategic nodes engaged

Stop measuring presence in one location. Ask: across this people's network, how many nodes have ongoing witness, and where are the gaps?
