Assumptions, model notes, and sources

How the dropdown works. Selecting a metro loads its population only. Birth rate, death rate, migration, and believer shares stay where you set them, so tune those for the city you picked. Canada runs lower on births and on believer share than the US defaults.

What this is. A gospel-burden model. It sizes the annual evangelistic need by counting every event that creates a need for a new believer: births (all counted as lost), non-believer net in-migration, and deaths. It is not a net population balance.

Gospel burden vs net floor. Gospel burden counts all deaths in full. The net floor subtracts the lost who die, since they also leave the pool, giving a conservative lower bound. The honest answer sits between the two.

The migration lever. Migration is set as a rate per 1,000 so it scales with population. The default reflects DFW's fast growth. International-heavy migration lowers the in-migrant believer share, which raises the figure.

An honest tension. Raising the believer share among the dying increases the replacement number but lowers the separated-from-God number. Pick one premise and stay consistent.

Definitions matter. A 23% believer share is self-identified evangelical, not practicing. State the definition whenever you cite a number from this tool.

Sources. Metro populations: U.S. Census Bureau 2024 metropolitan estimates and Statistics Canada 2024 census metropolitan area estimates. Believer shares: Pew Research Center 2023-24 Religious Landscape Study. Births: March of Dimes PeriStats. Baptism benchmark: Lifeway Research 2024 Annual Church Profile.
