How the math works, assumptions, and sources

The target moves with the population. The tool projects the population forward by births minus deaths (no migration), then solves for the steady annual baptisms needed so the evangelical share lands on the goal as a percent of the larger future population. Growing lostness is built into that growing denominator.

Every birth is counted as lost. No one is born evangelical. The evangelical population grows only through conversion and loses members through death at the group's death rate.

The goal options. Reach 2% and Reach 10% target that share of the future population. Increase by 2 points and Increase by 10 points add that many percentage points to the current share. A Reach target turns off once the group is already at or above that level, leaving the increase goals and the higher reach target available.

Zero base. A group reporting 0.00% evangelical is treated as 0.001% so the projection has a starting point. Even then the required growth rate runs to the hundreds of percent per year, which is the mathematical case for reproducing movements rather than one-by-one addition.

Data. You enter the population, percent evangelical, and percent Christian, typically from Joshua Project. Birth and death rates are demographic and come from the group's country (World Bank or UN). Unreached is 2% evangelical or below and 5% Christian adherent or below; Frontier is 0.1% Christian adherent or below. Figures are estimates for strategy and mobilization.
