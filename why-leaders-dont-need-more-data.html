Averages hide realities

Ahmed's homeland looked reached, but his cluster of villages had no believers, no churches, no missionaries. National and people-group averages buried whole untouched populations.

under 1% Christian
02

The problem is the wrong data

Most leaders do not lack information. They lack the right information at the right time. You can have 16,000+ unreached groups on a spreadsheet and still not know the next step.

16,000+ groups
03

Two metrics, too flat

Our shared language rests on Engaged or Unengaged and Christian percentage. They have helped, but they are too flat for the decisions we actually make.

04

Identical on paper, opposite in reality

Two groups, both engaged, both 0.2 percent evangelical. One has churches multiplying; the other is fragile and stalling. The numbers cannot tell them apart.

0.2% vs 0.2%
05

What the data cannot see

It creates false equivalence, cannot see momentum, masks on-the-ground health, does not name the next step, and is quietly shaped by funding incentives.

06

From numbers to clarity

We need dimensions that actually help: what phase a people is in, how strong and healthy the engagement is, and what moves them to the next phase.

“Averages can hide realities.”

“The Great Commission does not need more spreadsheets.”

I cannot give you perfect data. But I can help you find clarity in the complexity.
