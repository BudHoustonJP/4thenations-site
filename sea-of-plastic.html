The workers behind the plastic

Up to 130,000 migrants from Morocco, Romania, Colombia, Nigeria, and Ukraine form the labor backbone, clustered by nationality.

Believers already inside

Nigerian, Romanian, and Ukrainian evangelicals work alongside Muslim Moroccan and Algerian coworkers, with a proximity no outsider can replicate.

A node, not a dead end

Below-minimum wages, makeshift settlements, and 45 degree greenhouse heat. People economically fragile and far from home ask real questions about hope.

A circular pipeline

The GECCO program returns workers home after nine months. Discipleship during those months sends formation back to the home community.
