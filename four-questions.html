The four questions

Who are the people? What are they like? How can they be reached? How are we doing? Most teams answer question three first and skip the rest.

4 questions
02

Q1: who are the people

A team reaching South Asians in the Gulf, 10 million people, sharpened to Telugu-speaking Hindu laborers in Dubai construction.

10M to one segment
03

Clarity multiplies fruit

After two days defining who, they had a reproducing church within a year, not from working harder but from working with clarity.

1 year
04

Q2: three layers

Category knowledge from books, local knowledge from being there, and the insider view of how they see themselves. The breakthrough happens at layer three.

3 layers
05

Q3: strategy needs a foundation

Strategy without questions one and two is guesswork: wrong dialect, wrong networks, solving problems the group does not have.

06

Q4: how are we doing

One worker reached 0.2 percent in 15 years, a century or more to finish. Track both health and size, or you are lying to yourself.

0.2% in 15 years

“The time you do not spend on question one, you will spend later, usually multiplied by ten.”

“The differences within a category often matter more than the category itself.”

These are not bureaucratic hurdles. They are the foundation. And the order matters.
