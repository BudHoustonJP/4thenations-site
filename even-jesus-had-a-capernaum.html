Capernaum saw more miracles than any city in the ancient world. Chorazin and Bethsaida watched Jesus heal, teach, and feed crowds for three years. These were his primary operational bases. Yet in Luke 10, Jesus pronounces a Woe on all three.

What do we do when the primary node, the field that receives the most ministry attention, simply does not respond? The modern post-Christian Western city is a Capernaum problem. The moves Jesus made in response shape exactly how we should think about ours. Scroll through, and work the controls.

The receptivity paradox

Exposure and response ran in opposite directions

Jesus draws a contrast in Luke 10:13-15. The cities that saw the most would not turn. The pagan coast, given far less, would have repented in sackcloth and ashes. Tap any point to hear his comparison.

What Jesus was actually doing

The base existed to send, not to settle

The pattern

The node has not turned, but it still produces apostles

The city treats the gospel as ambient noise, yet it keeps forming individuals who can be sent. You do not abandon it and you do not pour everything back in hoping for corporate revival. You maintain the base, witness faithfully, find the remnant, and deploy them outward.

The trap

The field already reproduces, and we refuse to release

Local reproduction has been reached. The Spirit has done his work. The disciples bear fruit. Staying now is ministry perfectionism, and it stalls the frontier. This is the failure last week named. The Capernaum case is the opposite scenario, not a contradiction.

The Capernaum audit

Four questions for your team this week

Open each one. Read the reframe. The bar fills as you work through all four.

Are we measuring success by the city's corporate revival, or by the apostolic remnant the city is producing?

Have we identified the few who are ready to go? Are we forming them for deployment, or accidentally retaining them to prop up our local ministry?

Where are the bridges from our Capernaum to the global unreached frontier? Have we mapped the diaspora communities or training networks that can carry our remnant outward?

What is our exact plan to maintain a faithful presence here while simultaneously deploying our best people somewhere else?
